Movie Poster Design

Design a movie poster using the style of a specific designer or a design period.

Software Used: Adobe Indesign & Photoshop

Partnered with Ashran Bharosha

Scroll Down for Details & Analysis

Finished Project

Project Analysis

Design Movement

My partner and I decided to pursue the designer - Joseph Müller-Brockmann in the Swiss/International Design Movement.

Joseph Müller-Brockmann design principle focuses on the core message, clean and clear visual and grid for text. His work also represents a feeling of movement.

Color Selection

Color selection was a easy choice. The movie is about a taxi driver, thus we choose to use the primary color of yellow to represent the taxi.
With the black and white image of the taxi as the main focus, we also added leading lines to give the movie poster a sense of movement.

Font Selection

Joseph Müller-Brockmann mainly uses sans-serif fonts. Universe and Helvetica. So the fonts we chose was Open Sans for both headings and sub headings.

Image Selection

Using Adobe Photoshop, we deleted the background from the taxi, then we added a black & white filter along with posterized effect to give the overall image a vintage and further separate the layers of color.

Later, we used the brush tool to clean up the reflection and the white mark on the ground.

Layout Design

With the help of grid, we rotated the taxi and lined up the text. We also added some thin and thick line near the wheel of the taxi to create a feeling of movement.

Project Reflection

During this project, my partner and I sometimes has some disagreement. We both learned to listen and communicate our idea to each other then select which one suits the project better.
Something that I would change about this movie poster is to seperate the font weight and the font family of the headings and the subheadings. Right now there isn't enough difference between them so words blend together, the result is a bland poster.
